Introduction to Automated Trading

What is Automated Trading?

Automated trading, or algorithmic trading, uses computer programs to execute trades based on predefined strategies. While it offers speed and efficiency, it faces challenges like slow reactions to market changes, inflexibility, and the need for manual adjustments.

Disadvantages of Traditional ATS

  • Information delay
  • Inflexible
  • Labor-intensive

DQN in Trading (Timeline)

1. Process Market Data

The first step in DQN is to process the market data, define the state space, and establish actions and a reward function.

2. Experience Replay

During training, DQN uses experience replay, a method that stores past experiences and reuses them randomly, breaking the relationship between data points.

3. Simulate Trading

Finally, historical market data is used to simulate trading and refine strategies. This helps improve decision-making over time.

Advantages of DQN in Automated Trading

  • Fast iteration
  • Anytime and anywhere
  • Emotion-free
  • Complex pattern analysis

Challenges of Implementing DQN

  • Data delays in high-frequency trading (HFT) will cause the model to use outdated data for decision-making, leading to strategic errors.
  • Overfitting to historical data during backtesting can result in poor real-world performance.

Conclusion

By integrating DQN into automated trading systems, decision-making becomes faster, more adaptable, and data-driven. While challenges remain, DQN holds the potential to improve trading systems, making them more efficient and capable of navigating volatile markets.
